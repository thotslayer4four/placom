WAREHOUSE NETWORK

Modern Commodity Storage Infrastructure

PLACOM is building a statewide network of warehouses, aggregation centres, and cold storage facilities to reduce post-harvest losses, support commodity financing, and stabilise Plateau State’s agricultural markets.

INFRASTRUCTURE

Types of Warehousing Facilities

The PLACOM warehousing network is designed to serve different points across the commodity value chain — from farmgate collection to long-term storage and structured financing.

Aggregation Centres

Strategically located farmgate collection points where smallholder farmers consolidate produce before grading, weighing, and onward distribution. Reduces transportation costs and post-harvest losses at source.

Certified Public Warehouses

Government-backed, PLACOM-affiliated warehouses offering secure, insured storage for agricultural commodities. Certified warehouses enable the issuance of Warehouse Receipts used for commodity-backed financing.

Cold Storage Facilities

Temperature-controlled storage designed for high-value perishables including strawberries, tomatoes, Irish potatoes, vegetables, and dairy products — extending shelf life and market window.

Quality Control Laboratories

On-site grading, testing, and certification facilities ensuring commodities meet national and international standards before entering trading, financing, or export channels.

Warehouse Receipt Systems

A digital and paper-based receipting infrastructure that allows farmers to store commodities in certified warehouses and use the receipt as collateral to access credit — without selling at distress prices.

Public Food Reserve System

Strategic commodity reserves managed in partnership with government agencies to support price stabilisation, food security, and emergency supply management across Plateau State.

HOW IT WORKS

The Warehouse Receipt System

The Warehouse Receipt System (WRS) is a key financial innovation that allows farmers and traders to unlock the value of stored commodities without selling them prematurely at low prices.

Deposit commodity

Farmer delivers graded, certified commodities to a PLACOM-accredited warehouse.

Receive warehouse receipt

A certified warehouse receipt is issued, confirming the quantity, quality, and ownership of stored goods.

Access financing

The receipt is presented to a financial institution as collateral to access a loan or working capital.

Sell when prices are right

The farmer sells at a time and price of their choosing, repays the loan, and collects remaining proceeds.

BENEFITS

Why WRS Matters

  • Eliminates distress selling at harvest-time lows
  • Provides access to affordable credit for farmers
  • Reduces post-harvest losses through proper storage
  • Supports commodity price stabilisation
  • Creates structured, transparent commodity markets
  • Enables financial institutions to lend with confidence
