KEY COMMODITIES

What Plateau Produces

From premium highland produce to critical minerals — Plateau State's commodity base spans three distinct sectors, all flowing through PLACOM's integrated value chain.

AGRICULTURE

Irish Potatoes & Vegetables

Nigeria's largest highland produce sector — potatoes, tomatoes, strawberries, cabbage, carrots, and peas grown in Plateau's near-temperate climate.

GRAINS & LEGUMES

Soybean, Sorghum & Millet

Staple grains and legumes supporting domestic food security and feeding livestock and processing industries across Northern Nigeria.

SOLID MINERALS

Tin, Columbite & Gemstones

Plateau State's mineral belt includes cassiterite (tin ore), columbite, amethyst, topaz, limestone, and kaolin — with significant export and industrial value.

WAREHOUSING NETWORK

Modern Commodity Storage Infrastructure

PLACOM is promoting the development of a statewide warehousing network supporting commodity preservation, market stability, quality assurance, structured commodity financing, and public food reserve systems.

Aggregation Centres

Farmgate collection points where commodities are consolidated from smallholder producers across Plateau State.

Public Warehouses

Government-backed certified warehouses providing secure, insured storage for agricultural and mineral commodities.

Cold Storage Facilities

Temperature-controlled storage for perishable commodities including fruits, vegetables, and dairy products.

Warehouse Receipt Systems

Commodity-backed receipts enabling farmers to access credit using stored produce as collateral.

Quality Control Laboratories

On-site testing and certification ensuring commodities meet national and international quality standards.

PUBLIC FOOD PROCUREMENT & FOOD SECURITY

Structured Systems for Reliable Food Supply

PLACOM supports structured public food procurement systems that improve market access for smallholder farmers while strengthening food security and public grain reserve management. The ecosystem promotes local commodity sourcing, organized food systems, market stabilization, increased farmer participation, and sustainable food supply systems.
