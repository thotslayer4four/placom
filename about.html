ABOUT US

Our Story & Mission

The Plateau Commodity Market Company Limited (PLACOM) — established by Governor Barrister Caleb Mutfwang in September 2024 to drive integrated commodity marketing, supply chain management, and agro–solid minerals industrial facilitation across Plateau State.

OUR STORY

Established to Transform Agriculture & Minerals

Plateau Commodity Marketing Company Limited (PLACOM) is a State-owned Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) established to strengthen commodity marketing, warehousing, supply chain management and value chain development in Plateau State. The company was incorporated under the Companies and Allied Matters Act (CAMA) 2020 on 4th September 2024 as a private company limited by shares and is wholly owned by the Plateau State Government through designated state institutions.

Plateau State's abundant agricultural produce — Irish potatoes, strawberries, tomatoes, vegetables, grains — alongside rich solid mineral deposits and unique eco-tourism assets, lacked organized market systems. Farmers, artisanal miners, and tourism operators struggled with price volatility and limited access to premium markets.

PLACOM was created in response to the growing need for an organized commodity marketing structure capable of connecting producers to profitable markets, reducing post-harvest losses, supporting industrial development and improving livelihoods across the state. The company serves as a strategic platform for the commercialization of agricultural commodities, solid minerals, tourism-related products and other strategic resources produced within Plateau State.

WHY PLACOM WAS ESTABLISHED

Addressing the Gaps in Plateau State's Commodity Economy

The global economy is increasingly driven by sustainable industrial development, food security, climate resilience and organized commodity systems. Plateau State possesses enormous natural resource potential including fertile agricultural land, vast solid mineral deposits, tourism and eco-tourism assets, and strategic geographical advantages.

Despite these opportunities, producers have historically faced significant challenges:

Poor Market Access

Limited connections between producers and profitable domestic and international markets.

Weak Supply Chain Systems

Fragmented aggregation, transportation and distribution infrastructure across the state.

Huge Post-Harvest Losses

Inadequate storage infrastructure leading to significant commodity wastage after harvest.

Price Instability

Commodity price volatility leaving farmers and miners exposed to unpredictable income.

Limited Organized Market Access

Lack of structured commodity exchanges and trading platforms accessible to local producers.

Quality & Standards Gap

Absence of commodity grading and certification systems reducing competitiveness in premium markets.

PLACOM was established to address these gaps by creating a structured commodity ecosystem that supports production, aggregation, warehousing, processing, marketing and exports — driving inclusive economic growth, strengthening food security and facilitating industrialization through efficient value chain development.

KEY OBJECTIVES

Eight pillars of PLACOM's institutional mandate.

01 — Commodity Market Development

Establish organized, transparent commodity marketing systems that connect Plateau State's producers to domestic and international buyers.

02 — Digital Trading Platform

Build a technology-enabled commodity exchange and real-time price information system accessible to farmers, traders, and buyers statewide.

03 — Structured Commodity Finance

Develop warehouse receipt systems and commodity-backed financing mechanisms that give farmers access to affordable credit against stored produce.

04 — Cooperative Registration

Register, develop, and empower farmer and artisanal miner cooperatives statewide, building collective market power and shared infrastructure.

05 — Agro-Processing Zones

Establish processing facilities that transform raw commodities into higher-value products, creating manufacturing employment and retaining value within Plateau State.

06 — Export Facilitation

Open international trade channels, achieve export certification standards, and connect Plateau State commodities to premium buyers across Africa, Europe, and the Middle East.

07 — Solid Minerals Marketing

Organize and commercialize Plateau State's mineral resource base — tin, columbite, gemstones, and industrial minerals — through structured, compliant market channels.

08 — Price Stabilization

Protect farmers and miners from commodity price volatility through market intervention mechanisms, buffer stocks, and structured financial instruments.
