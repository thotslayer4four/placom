Connecting Plateau Commodities to Global Markets

TRADE FRAMEWORKS

Market Access Through Key Trade Agreements

Continental Framework

AfCFTA — African Continental Free Trade Area

Nigeria is a signatory to AfCFTA, giving PLACOM and its partners preferential access to markets across 54 African nations. PLACOM is positioning Plateau State’s commodities to take advantage of reduced tariffs and regional supply chain integration under this framework.

US Preferential Access

AGOA — African Growth and Opportunity Act

Under AGOA, eligible Nigerian agricultural commodities can enter the United States market duty-free. PLACOM supports export-certification, packaging standards, and documentation to help Plateau State producers access this preferential trade channel.

HOW PLACOM HELPS

End-to-End Export Facilitation

Export Certification

Grading, quality testing, and certification of commodities to meet destination country import standards and buyer requirements.

Trade Documentation

Support with export documentation including phytosanitary certificates, certificates of origin, packing lists, and trade compliance paperwork.

Logistics Coordination

Coordination of transport from Plateau State warehouses to ports, including cold chain logistics for perishable commodities.

International Buyer Connections

Facilitating direct connections between Plateau State producers and verified international buyers, importers, and distribution networks.

Commodity Exchange Access

Connecting exporters to structured trading through the NCX, AFEX, and Lagos Commodities and Futures Exchange for price discovery and export contracts.

Trade Finance Support

Working with financial institutions to provide letters of credit, export finance, and commodity-backed lending to support export transactions.
