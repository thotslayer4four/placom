ECO-TOURISM FACILITATION

Plateau State's Natural Heritage

Nigeria's "Home on the Hills" — Plateau State's unique highland landscapes, wildlife reserves, and cultural heritage represent an untapped economic sector that PLACOM is positioned to facilitate.

PLATEAU'S ADVANTAGE

Nigeria's Only Temperate Plateau

At over 1,200 metres above sea level, Plateau State enjoys a near-temperate climate unlike any other Nigerian state. This distinct environment sustains ecosystems, wildlife, and natural landscapes that offer world-class eco-tourism experiences — and PLACOM's mandate includes connecting tourism operators to organized markets and development infrastructure.

KEY DESTINATIONS

Plateau State's Tourism Assets

Five signature destinations — from ancient rock formations to West African wildlife parks — form the core of Plateau State's eco-tourism offer.

LANDMARK

Shere Hills

Ancient granite inselbergs rising dramatically from the Jos Plateau. Popular for hiking, rock climbing, and panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

NATURAL WONDER

Assop Falls

A spectacular waterfall set in lush highland forest. One of Nigeria's most scenic eco-tourism destinations, drawing visitors for its natural beauty and cool microclimate.

CONSERVATION

Jos Wildlife Park

One of Nigeria's premier wildlife parks, home to a diverse range of African wildlife species. A conservation landmark and major draw for domestic and international eco-tourists.

HERITAGE SITE

Wase Rock

A dramatic isolated rock formation with historical and cultural significance. An ancient natural fortress that draws visitors for its striking appearance and storied past.

GAME RESERVE

Pandam Game Reserve

A protected wildlife conservation area offering safari experiences and wildlife observation in a natural savanna-woodland ecosystem unique to the Plateau region.

GEOLOGICAL LANDMARK

Riyom Rock Formation

A unique geological landmark and iconic symbol of Plateau State's rich natural heritage — offering striking visual appeal and eco-tourism potential.

CULTURAL HERITAGE & FESTIVALS

A Richly Diverse Cultural Tapestry

Plateau State is culturally diverse, with rich traditions, arts, crafts, music and festivals celebrated by its many ethnic communities. These cultural assets create opportunities for tourism-driven economic growth and community development.

Traditional Festivals

Annual cultural festivals celebrated across Plateau State's diverse ethnic communities, drawing visitors from across Nigeria and beyond.

Indigenous Arts & Crafts

Locally crafted artefacts, textiles, pottery, and artisan products reflecting the state's rich creative heritage and cultural identity.

Local Cuisines

Distinctive highland food culture featuring fresh local produce, traditional recipes, and culinary experiences unique to Plateau State.

Community Tourism

Immersive community tourism experiences that connect visitors directly with local life, traditions, and economic activities.

Cultural Exhibitions

Curated showcases of Plateau State's heritage, history, and cultural diversity for educational and tourism purposes.

AGRI-TOURISM & COMMODITY TOURISM

Where Agriculture Meets the Visitor Economy

PLACOM promotes the integration of tourism with agriculture and commodity development, strengthening market visibility for Plateau-branded commodities while growing tourism and investment opportunities.

Farm Tourism Experiences

Guided farm visits, harvest experiences, and agricultural learning programmes across Plateau State's key production zones.

Commodity Exhibitions

Trade fairs and commodity showcases that position Plateau produce and minerals in front of investors, buyers, and development partners.

Agricultural Festivals

Seasonal agricultural festivals celebrating Plateau's harvest culture and providing market platforms for farmers and traders.

Plateau Commodity Showcases

Dedicated events promoting awareness of Plateau-branded commodities — Irish potatoes, strawberries, ginger, and minerals — to national and international audiences.

ECONOMIC OPPORTUNITY

Tourism as an economic sector.

Local Employment

Tourism creates jobs across hospitality, transport, guiding, and craft sectors — distributing income directly to communities near attractions.

Revenue Diversification

Tourism reduces dependence on primary commodity sectors and provides resilient, year-round income streams for Plateau State's economy.

International Exposure

Eco-tourism attracts international visitors who become ambassadors for Plateau State's agricultural and mineral commodities in their home markets.

MSME Growth

Tourism spending supports micro and small enterprises — food vendors, craft producers, accommodation providers, and local guides — across the tourism corridor.

PLACOM'S ROLE IN TOURISM

Connecting Tourism Operators to Organized Markets

PLACOM's mandate explicitly includes eco-tourism facilitation as a primary economic sector alongside agriculture and solid minerals. This recognizes that Plateau State's natural heritage is an economic asset that requires the same organized marketing and institutional support as its commodity sectors.

Through PLACOM, tourism operators gain access to development finance, organized marketing platforms, capacity building, and connections to domestic and international tourism networks — transforming Plateau's natural heritage into a structured, investable economic sector.
