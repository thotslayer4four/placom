MARKET ACCESS & EXPORT POTENTIAL

Gateway to Regional & International Markets

PLACOM supports access to domestic commodity markets, regional trade opportunities, organized commodity exchanges, and international commodity networks. The company is positioned to support scalable trade and investment operations under AfCFTA and AGOA — opening regional export markets across Africa, Europe, and beyond.

AfCFTA Alignment

Leverage the African Continental Free Trade Area to access duty-reduced commodity trade across 54 African nations.

AGOA Market Access

Export eligible agricultural commodities to the United States under preferential duty-free terms through the African Growth and Opportunity Act.

Commodity Exchanges

Access structured commodity trading through the NCX, AFEX, and Lagos Commodities and Futures Exchange for transparent price discovery and contract execution.

Institutional Buyers

STRUCTURED FINANCE

Warehouse Receipt Systems & Price Stabilization

PLACOM's warehouse infrastructure does more than store commodities — it creates a financial ecosystem that unlocks credit for farmers, stabilizes prices, and gives institutional investors a bankable asset class backed by certified commodity stocks.

Warehouse Receipt Finance

PLACOM-certified warehouses issue receipts against stored commodities. Farmers and cooperatives use these receipts as collateral to access affordable working capital loans from financial institutions — without selling at harvest-time low prices.

Price Stabilization Mechanisms

PLACOM operates market intervention mechanisms that smooth seasonal price swings. By holding certified buffer stocks and timing market releases strategically, PLACOM protects farmers from post-harvest price crashes and consumers from shortage-driven spikes.

Commodity-Backed Investment

For institutional investors, PLACOM's commodity receipt infrastructure creates a new asset class: certified, insured, and physically-backed commodity positions in Nigerian agricultural markets. A bankable alternative to direct commodity ownership.
